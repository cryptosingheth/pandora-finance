★ European Union · ESPR

Product transparency is becoming the law.

Under the EU's Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation, a Digital Product Passport will be mandatory for a growing list of categories — starting with batteries, then textiles, electronics and more. Brands selling into Europe will be legally required to make provenance, materials and sustainability data verifiable.

The DPP Company was built for exactly this shift. Issue compliant, standards-aligned passports today and stay ahead of every deadline — instead of scrambling when enforcement lands.

  • 2027Batteries lead the wayThe battery passport is first to require a DPP under EU rules.
  • 2027+Textiles & apparelAmong the first priority groups slated for DPP requirements.
  • NextElectronics, furniture & morePriority categories expand across the ESPR rollout.
  • NowGet ahead of itStart issuing verifiable passports before enforcement begins.
How it works

From product to proof in three steps.

No blockchain expertise required. The DPP Company handles issuance, anchoring and the consumer experience end to end.

Brand creates a passport

Add product details, materials, provenance, certifications and care instructions. Media is stored on IPFS and the passport is minted on-chain — one per item or per batch.

Issue & anchor

Product gets a QR / NFC tag

Each passport links to a QR code or NFC chip attached to the product, its label or packaging. One tap or scan opens the live, verifiable passport — no app to install.

Tag & ship

Consumer scans & verifies

Buyers instantly confirm authenticity, trace provenance and see sustainability and care details. Genuine ownership can transfer to the new owner when the item changes hands.
