The problem

Ontario's rental market fails both sides at once

Newcomers can't prove they're reliable, and owners can't be sure they'll get paid. The gap between them is filled with cash deposits, guesswork, and risk. Propty closes it.

VS
For tenants

Newcomers get locked out

  • No Canadian credit history. A landlord's credit check returns nothing, so a qualified newcomer looks like a risk on paper.
  • No rental history to show. First apartment in Canada means no references, no track record, no way to prove reliability.
  • Deposit exploitation. Pushed to pay 6–12 months of rent upfront just to be considered — a barrier that is not legal under the RTA, but happens anyway.
  • Unfair, opaque screening. Decisions made behind closed doors, with no clear criteria and no way to build a case for yourself.
For owners & operators

Housing newcomers feels risky

  • Payment risk. With no credit signal to lean on, owners can't tell a reliable tenant from a costly one — so many won't rent to newcomers at all.
  • The LTB backlog. If a tenancy goes wrong, a Landlord and Tenant Board hearing can take 6–12 months — carrying an unpaid unit the entire time.
  • Damage & turnover risk. Rooms and shared homes turn over often, and one bad arrangement can wipe out months of rent.
  • Grey-market rent-to-rent. Room rentals often happen off the books, with handshake deals that protect nobody when something breaks.

Built for compliance

Compliance isn't the fine print — it's the product
